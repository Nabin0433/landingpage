Our Solution

One of the major challenges facing the wind energy industry is the low efficiency and high production costs of many existing wind turbine designs.

These turbines often struggle to generate sufficiently power in low wind conditions, leading to higher operating costs and reduced profitability.

A key technical limitation of traditional wind turbine designs is their reliance on thick, heavy blades that have a high mechanical resistance when rotating. This results in lower efficiency and higher production costs, as the turbines struggle to generate sufficient power in low wind conditions.

DATA AND EVIDENCE

  • We have testing data that compares the performance of our high-performance flat and thin wind turbines with existing designs.
  • For example, our new design has an efficiency of 0.54, significantly higher than the 0.5 efficiencies of many traditional designs. This improved efficiency translates into higher power generation and lower operating costs for our customers.
  • The higher efficiency of our new wind turbine design means that it is able to generate more power in low wind conditions, resulting in a more consistent and reliable source of power.
  • This, in turn, leads to lower operating costs for our customers, as they are able to generate more power with the same amount of wind energy.

PERFROMANCE

In addition to the immediate benefits of higher power generation and lower operating costs, the improved efficiency of our new wind turbine design also leads to long-term benefits.

For example, it allows for the use of smaller generators, which reduces the upfront costs of adoption and makes our turbine more accessible to a wider range of customers.

The reduced production costs of our new wind turbine design also contribute to increased profitability for our customers.

The flat, thin wings used in our design are lighter and less expensive to produce than traditional materials, which means that our turbines are more cost-effective to manufacture and maintain.
